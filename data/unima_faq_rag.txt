University of Malawi (UNIMA) – FAQ Knowledge Base for RAG Systems

Q: What is the University of Malawi?
A: The University of Malawi (UNIMA) is one of the oldest and most prestigious higher education institutions in Malawi, established in 1965.

Q: Where is UNIMA located?
A: UNIMA has multiple campuses across Malawi, including Zomba and Blantyre.

Q: What programs does UNIMA offer?
A: UNIMA offers undergraduate and postgraduate programs in fields such as education, humanities, science, business, and law.

Q: What are the admission requirements?
A: Admission typically requires Malawi School Certificate of Education (MSCE) or equivalent, with competitive selection based on merit.

Q: How can students apply?
A: Applications are usually submitted through the central university selection system or directly via official UNIMA channels.

Q: Does UNIMA offer accommodation?
A: Yes, limited on-campus accommodation is available, allocated based on criteria such as distance and need.

Q: What is the academic year structure?
A: The academic year is typically divided into two semesters.

Q: Are there online learning options?
A: Some programs and courses may offer blended or online learning options depending on the faculty.

Q: What facilities are available?
A: UNIMA provides libraries, laboratories, ICT services, and student support services.

Q: How can students contact the university?
A: Students can contact UNIMA through official communication channels including email, phone, or the university website.
