UNIVERSITY OF MALAWI (UNIMA) - STUDENT REGISTRATION INFORMATION
=================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
Student registration is the process by which students officially enroll in courses and programs at the University of Malawi (UNIMA) for each academic semester. All students must complete registration before attending classes or sitting for examinations.

---

ACADEMIC CALENDAR & REGISTRATION PERIODS
-----------------------------------------
- First Semester Registration: Typically takes place in August/September before the semester begins.
- Second Semester Registration: Typically takes place in January/February before the semester begins.
- Late Registration Period: A grace period of two (2) weeks is allowed after the official registration deadline, subject to a late registration fee.
- Students are advised to check the official UNIMA academic calendar each year for exact dates.

---

WHO NEEDS TO REGISTER
----------------------
- All new (fresh) students admitted to undergraduate programs.
- All continuing undergraduate students.
- All postgraduate students (Masters and PhD).
- Students returning from deferment or medical leave.
- Students who were on academic probation and have been reinstated.

---

REGISTRATION REQUIREMENTS
--------------------------
Before registering, students must ensure they have the following:

1. Proof of payment of tuition and other required fees for the semester.
2. A valid student ID card (or admission letter for new students).
3. Clearance from the Finance/Bursary Office confirming no outstanding balance.
4. Clearance from the Library (for continuing students) confirming no overdue books or fines.
5. Accommodation confirmation (if residing in university hostels).
6. Academic advisor approval of course selection (where applicable).

---

HOW TO REGISTER - STEP BY STEP
--------------------------------
Step 1: Pay your tuition and semester fees at the designated bank or via the UNIMA online payment portal.
Step 2: Obtain a payment receipt/confirmation from the Finance/Bursary Office.
Step 3: Visit the Registrar's Office or log in to the UNIMA Student Portal to access the registration form.
Step 4: Select your courses for the semester, ensuring they meet your program requirements.
Step 5: Get your course selection approved by your Head of Department or Academic Advisor.
Step 6: Submit your completed registration form to the Registrar's Office or finalize online.
Step 7: Collect your official registration card/slip as proof of registration.

---

ONLINE REGISTRATION
--------------------
UNIMA provides an online student portal for registration. Students can:
- Log in using their student number and password.
- View available courses for their program and year of study.
- Select and register for courses online.
- Print or download their registration slip upon completion.
- Check registration status and course timetables.

For assistance with the portal, contact the ICT Helpdesk or visit the Registrar's Office.

---

COURSE REGISTRATION RULES
---------------------------
- Students must register for the minimum and maximum credit load as specified by their faculty.
- Undergraduate students typically register for 5 to 7 courses per semester.
- Students must not register for courses that clash in the timetable.
- Pre-requisite courses must be passed before registering for advanced courses.
- Students may only register for courses offered in their program unless cross-faculty enrollment is approved.
- Auditing a course (attending without credit) requires written permission from the Dean.

---

ADDING AND DROPPING COURSES
-----------------------------
- Students may add or drop courses within the first two (2) weeks of the semester without academic penalty.
- After the add/drop period, students must apply for a course withdrawal through the Registrar's Office.
- Late withdrawal (after week 6) may result in a "W" (Withdrawn) grade on the transcript.
- Dropping below the minimum credit load requires Dean's approval.

---

LATE REGISTRATION
------------------
- Students who fail to register by the official deadline may register during the late registration period.
- A late registration fee of MK 5,000 (subject to change) is charged.
- Students who do not register by the end of the late registration period will not be allowed to attend classes or sit examinations for that semester.
- Exceptions may be granted for medical or other emergencies with supporting documentation.

---

DEFERMENT OF STUDIES
---------------------
- A student who is unable to register for a semester may apply to defer their studies.
- Deferment applications must be submitted to the Registrar's Office before or at the start of the semester.
- Deferment is allowed for a maximum of two (2) consecutive semesters.
- Students on deferment must notify the Registrar's Office when they wish to resume studies and re-register.

---

REGISTRATION FOR POSTGRADUATE STUDENTS
----------------------------------------
- Postgraduate students must register at the beginning of each academic year.
- In addition to course registration, postgraduate students must register their research topic/thesis proposal with the School of Graduate Studies.
- Supervisors must be confirmed and approved before research registration is finalized.
- Postgraduate students must also clear any outstanding fees before registration is processed.

---

FEES RELATED TO REGISTRATION
------------------------------
- Tuition fees vary by faculty and program. Refer to the Fees Structure document for detailed amounts.
- Other charges include: Student Union fee, Library fee, Medical/Health fee, ICT fee, and Examination fee.
- All fees must be paid before or during registration.
- Installment payment plans may be available — contact the Bursary Office for details.
- Scholarships and bursaries: Students with government or institutional sponsorship must bring their sponsorship letter to the Bursary Office before registration.

---

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS (FAQs) - REGISTRATION
--------------------------------------------------

Q: What happens if I miss the registration deadline?
A: You may register during the late registration period with an additional fee. After that period, you will not be permitted to attend classes or sit exams until the next semester.

Q: Can I register without paying fees?
A: No. Full or agreed partial payment of fees is required before registration can be processed.

Q: How do I know which courses to register for?
A: Consult your program handbook and speak with your academic advisor or Head of Department for guidance on required and elective courses.

Q: I lost my registration slip. What do I do?
A: Visit the Registrar's Office with your student ID to request a duplicate registration slip.

Q: Can I register for courses from another faculty?
A: Cross-faculty registration is possible but requires written approval from both your Dean and the Dean of the host faculty.

Q: What do I do if a course I need is full?
A: Contact your Head of Department. In some cases, class capacity may be increased or an alternative section may be arranged.

Q: How do I change my registered courses after registration?
A: You may add or drop courses within the first two weeks of semester. Visit the Registrar's Office or use the student portal to make changes.

Q: Who do I contact if I have problems with online registration?
A: Contact the ICT Helpdesk or visit the Registrar's Office for assistance.

IMPORTANT REMINDERS
---------------------
- Always keep a copy of your registration slip — it is proof of your enrollment.
- Attend all classes from the first day of semester, even before registration is finalized.
- Failure to register does not exempt a student from fee obligations for that semester if they have attended classes.
- Registration details are used for official university records, transcripts, and graduation eligibility.
