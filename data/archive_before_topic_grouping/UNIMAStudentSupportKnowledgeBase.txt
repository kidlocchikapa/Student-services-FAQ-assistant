Q: Where is the University of Malawi located?
A: The University is located in Zomba, the old capital city of Malawi. It is about 2km from Zomba's main trading centre at a location called Chirunga. Zomba is a short drive away from Blantyre, Malawi's largest commercial city.

Q: What qualifications does the University of Malawi offer?
A: The main qualifications offered are Bachelor's degrees in a range of disciplines, which normally take 4 years to complete. The university also offers postgraduate programmes at Masters and PhD level, as well as short training courses leading to certificates.

Q: How do I apply for admission to UNIMA?
A: Applications can be made through the following portals depending on the programme:
- Undergraduate (UCE): https://admission.unima.ac.mw
- Postgraduate: https://admission.unima.ac.mw
- Law programmes: https://clea.unima.ac.mw
- Public University Application: https://pus.nche.ac.mw
- Economic Fee-paying and Mature Entry application forms are also available on the UNIMA website.

Q: What are the requirements for international students to be admitted?
A: International students should contact the University directly for specific admission requirements. The admissions office can be reached via the student portal or by contacting the Registrar at registrar@unima.ac.mw.

Q: Does UNIMA offer online learning?
A: Yes. UNIMA offers online learning through the following platforms:
- Moodle e-learning platform: https://elearning.unima.ac.mw
- ECampus (fully online degrees): https://unima.ecampus.ac.mw
- ODeL Department for open and distance learning.

Q: What are the official lecture hours at UNIMA?
A: The official periods of operation are from 7:30am to 4:30pm, Monday to Friday. The University also offers weekend courses, and staff and students may arrange lectures or discussions outside these hours.

Q: How do I access my results or academic transcripts?
A: Academic results and transcripts can be accessed through the student portal at https://students.unima.ac.mw. For transcript verification requests, send an email to registrarverifications@unima.ac.mw.

Q: Are there internship or attachment opportunities for students?
A: Yes. Various faculties and departments occasionally offer students opportunities to be attached to selected organisations during the vacation period. Students may also assist in research projects run by members of staff. Organisations may also invite student applications for attachment opportunities.

Q: Can I transfer credits from another institution?
A: Yes, UNIMA has a credit transfer policy. Students should contact the admissions team with their transcripts for evaluation.

Q: Does UNIMA offer counselling services?
A: Yes. Counselling services are available at three locations:
- The University Chaplain for spiritual and personal matters.
- The Dean of Students for general welfare issues.
- The Psychology Department for students experiencing mental health difficulties.
- All services are confidential.

Q: Who do I contact for non-academic personal issues?
A: The Director of Student Affairs and your personal tutor are the first points of contact for non-academic matters. They can advise on issues ranging from accommodation to social life at the university.

Q: Does UNIMA accommodate students with disabilities?
A: Yes. UNIMA accommodates students with disabilities and has improved infrastructure for physical accessibility. There is a Special Needs Section housed in the ground floor of the main library building, which provides resources including materials in Braille for visually impaired students. Staff and students are also sensitized to avoid discrimination against students with disabilities. Room allocation gives preference to students with disabilities.

Q: Is there a health clinic on campus?
A: Yes. UNIMA has an on-campus clinic. More information is available at https://unima.ac.mw/clinic.

Q: Where can students access food on campus?
A: The main dining facility is the University cafeteria where students can purchase food at affordable prices. There are also privately run restaurants around campus.

Q: What other facilities are available on campus?
A: Campus facilities include:
- A main library with special needs resources.
- The Great Hall for events and ceremonies.
- A clinic for medical services.
- Chanco Community Radio and Chanco TV.
- Sports and recreation facilities.
- Places of worship for spiritual life.

Q: Are there student clubs and organizations at UNIMA?
A: Yes. UNIMA offers a vibrant student life with various clubs and societies covering academic, cultural, and sports interests. Every student automatically becomes a member of the Students Representative Council (SRC), formerly known as the Students Union of Chancellor College (SUCC).

Q: What sports and recreational activities are available?
A: The University provides sports and recreation facilities and activities for students. More information is available under "Student Sports & Recreation" on the UNIMA website.

Q: Is there spiritual support available for students?
A: Yes. UNIMA supports student spiritual life and has facilities and organizations for students of different faiths. The University Chaplain is also available for consultation on spiritual matters.
