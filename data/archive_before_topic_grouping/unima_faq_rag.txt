Q: What is the University of Malawi?
A: The University of Malawi (UNIMA) is one of the oldest higher education institutions in Malawi. It was established in 1965.

Q: Where is UNIMA located?
A: UNIMA's main campus is in Zomba. The university also has other campuses in Malawi, including Blantyre.

Q: What programs does UNIMA offer?
A: UNIMA offers undergraduate and postgraduate programmes in fields such as education, humanities, science, business, and law.

Q: What are the admission requirements?
A: Admission requires the Malawi School Certificate of Education (MSCE) or an equivalent qualification. Selection is competitive and based on merit.

Q: How can students apply?
A: Students can apply through the central university selection system or through official UNIMA application channels.

Q: Does UNIMA offer accommodation?
A: Yes. UNIMA offers limited on-campus accommodation. Allocation is based on criteria such as distance and need.

Q: What is the academic year structure?
A: The academic year is divided into two semesters.

Q: Are there online learning options?
A: Some programmes and courses offer blended or online learning options, depending on the faculty.

Q: What facilities are available?
A: UNIMA provides libraries, laboratories, ICT services, and student support services.

Q: How can students contact the university?
A: Students can contact UNIMA through official channels such as email, phone, or the university website.
