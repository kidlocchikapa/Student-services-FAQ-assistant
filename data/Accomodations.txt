Q: Does the University of Malawi (UNIMA) provide accommodation for all students?
A: No. Students are admitted on a non-residential basis, which means accommodation is not guaranteed for everyone.

Q: Is accommodation guaranteed for first-year students?
A: No. Even first-year students are not guaranteed campus accommodation due to limited space.

Q: How can I get a room on campus?
A: Students must apply for hostel space. Rooms are limited and usually allocated through a computer-based random selection system. Priority is often given to students with disabilities.

Q: How are students selected for hostels?
A: Selection is done through a random computerized system, with males and females selected separately.

Q: What should I do if I don’t get campus accommodation?
A: Students must look for off-campus housing such as private flats, shared houses, or private student hostels near campus.

Q: Are there hostels available at UNIMA?
A: Yes, but they are limited. Rooms are shared and usually include basic furniture, but students must bring their own bedding.

Q: How many students are in one room at UNIMA hostels?
A: Two students per room.

Q: What furniture is provided in the rooms?
A: Beds, chairs for each student, and lockers are provided.

Q: Are bathrooms shared or private?
A: Bathrooms are shared among students.

Q: Is Wi-Fi available in UNIMA hostels?
A: Yes, Wi-Fi is available through eduroam.

Q: Are there study areas in the accommodation blocks?
A: Yes, there are study areas such as roundtables for group and individual study.

Q: Can students cook in UNIMA hostel rooms?
A: No. Cooking is not allowed in hostel rooms.

Q: What are the hostel curfew rules?
A: Students must stop movement and visiting by 00:00 (midnight).

Q: Are visitors allowed in the hostels?
A: Yes, but only after following necessary hostel procedures and rules.

Q: What happens if a student breaks hostel rules?
A: The student may be removed from accommodation.

Q: Who do students report maintenance issues to?
A: The maintenance department.

Q: What should a student do if something in their room breaks?
A: Report it to the maintenance department for repair or assistance.

Q: Is security provided in UNIMA hostels?
A: Yes. Security is provided 24 hours a day with CCTV surveillance and security personnel.

Q: When do students move into hostels?
A: Students usually move in on Sunday before the official Monday opening day.

Q: What should students bring when moving into hostels?
A: Students should bring beddings and personal belongings.

Q: When do students vacate hostel rooms?
A: Students vacate rooms on Saturday after the Friday closing of the semester or academic period.

Q: Can students choose their roommate?
A: No. Room allocation is random and system-based.

Q: Can students switch rooms after allocation?
A: Room changes are generally not allowed unless special approval is given.

Q: What happens if a student does not pay accommodation fees on time?
A: The student may lose hostel space or be denied access to accommodation.

Q: Are accommodation fees refundable?
A: No. Fees are not refundable if a student leaves or withdraws.

Q: How safe is UNIMA accommodation?
A: It is generally safe with 24-hour security, CCTV, and controlled access.

Q: Are electricity and water always available?
A: They are available, but occasional interruptions may occur depending on supply.

Q: Is there laundry support in hostels?
A: No formal laundry service. Students usually wash their own clothes.

Q: Do students keep the same room every year?
A: No. Students usually reapply each academic year.

Q: Why do students prefer campus accommodation?
A: Because it is cheaper, closer to classes, has a school environment, easy access to libraries, and cafeteria services.

Q: What is the biggest advantage of campus accommodation?
A: Better security, short distance to facilities, clean rooms, fewer disturbances, and no risk of eviction due to rent issues

Q: What do students do if they are not allocated campus accommodation?
A: They stay in private hostels, flats, and rented houses in areas near campus.

Q: Which areas are commonly used for private accommodation?
A: Chikanda, Naisi, Mpondabwino, Tholo, and Malawi Housing Corporation (MHC) flats.

Q: Why is Chikanda popular for student accommodation?
A: It is affordable, has local markets, and hosts a large student population of over 10,000 students.

Q: How far is Chikanda from campus?
A: It is very close, about 5–10 minutes walking distance.

Q: How far are other private areas from campus?
A: Areas like MHC flats are farther, so students use transport such as buses or motorcycles.

Q: Are private hostels safe?
A: They are generally safe, but students are responsible for their own safety and belongings.

Q: What type of rooms are available in private hostels?
A: Rooms can be single, double, or triple, but most commonly are double rooms.

Q: How do students pay rent in private hostels?
A: Rent is paid directly to landlords, usually in cash or bank transfer. There is no university involvement.

Q: Are private hostels cheaper than campus accommodation?
A: It depends on location and personal needs, but Chikanda is generally cheaper.

Q: Do private hostels have electricity and water?
A: It depends on cost. Higher-cost places are more reliable than cheaper ones.

Q: Do private hostels have rules like curfew?
A: It depends on the landlord. Some have strict rules, others are flexible.

Q: What problems do students face in private accommodation?
A: Theft and safety risks are common. Students must take care of their belongings and are advised to stay in groups in some areas.

Q: Why do students prefer campus accommodation over private housing?
A: Campus offers security, short distance, structured environment, library access, cafeteria services, and fewer financial risks.

Q: Do students need transport from private areas?
A: It depends. Chikanda is walkable, while farther areas use motorcycles or buses.
