Q: Does the University of Malawi (UNIMA) provide accommodation for all students?
A: No. UNIMA admits students on a non-residential basis, so accommodation is not guaranteed for every student.

Q: Is accommodation guaranteed for first-year students?
A: No. First-year students are not guaranteed campus accommodation because hostel space is limited.

Q: How can I get a room on campus?
A: Students must apply for hostel space. Rooms are limited and are usually allocated through a computerized random selection system. Priority may be given to students with disabilities.

Q: How are students selected for hostels?
A: Hostel selection is done through a computerized random system, with male and female students selected separately.

Q: What should I do if I do not get campus accommodation?
A: You should look for off-campus housing such as private flats, shared houses, or private student hostels near campus.

Q: Are there hostels available at UNIMA?
A: Yes. UNIMA has hostels, but the available space is limited. Rooms are shared and usually include basic furniture. Students must bring their own bedding.

Q: How many students stay in one room in UNIMA hostels?
A: Two students share one room.

Q: What furniture is provided in hostel rooms?
A: The rooms usually include beds, chairs for each student, and lockers.

Q: Are bathrooms shared or private in UNIMA hostels?
A: Bathrooms are shared.

Q: Is Wi-Fi available in UNIMA hostels?
A: Yes. Wi-Fi is available through eduroam.

Q: Are there study areas in the accommodation blocks?
A: Yes. There are study areas, including round tables for group and individual study.

Q: Can students cook in UNIMA hostel rooms?
A: No. Cooking is not allowed in hostel rooms.

Q: What are the hostel curfew rules?
A: Students must stop movement and visiting by 00:00 (midnight).

Q: Are visitors allowed in the hostels?
A: Yes, but visitors must follow hostel procedures and rules.

Q: What happens if a student breaks hostel rules?
A: The student may be removed from the accommodation.

Q: What should a student do if something in their room breaks?
A: The student should report it to the maintenance department for repair or assistance.

Q: Is security provided in UNIMA hostels?
A: Yes. Security is provided 24 hours a day with CCTV surveillance and security personnel.

Q: When do students move into hostels?
A: Students usually move in on Sunday before the official Monday opening day.

Q: What should students bring when moving into hostels?
A: Students should bring bedding and their personal belongings.

Q: When do students vacate hostel rooms?
A: Students usually vacate their rooms on Saturday after the Friday closing of the semester or academic period.

Q: Can students choose their roommate?
A: No. Room allocation is random and system-based.

Q: Can students switch rooms after allocation?
A: Room changes are generally not allowed unless special approval is given.

Q: What happens if a student does not pay accommodation fees on time?
A: The student may lose hostel space or be denied access to accommodation.

Q: Are accommodation fees refundable?
A: No. Accommodation fees are not refundable if a student leaves or withdraws.

Q: How safe is UNIMA accommodation?
A: UNIMA accommodation is generally safe because it has 24-hour security, CCTV, and controlled access.

Q: Are electricity and water always available in the hostels?
A: Electricity and water are available, but occasional interruptions may happen depending on supply.

Q: Is there laundry support in UNIMA hostels?
A: No formal laundry service is provided. Students usually wash their own clothes.

Q: Do students keep the same room every year?
A: No. Students usually reapply for accommodation each academic year.

Q: Why do students prefer campus accommodation?
A: Many students prefer campus accommodation because it is cheaper, closer to classes, offers a school environment, and provides easy access to libraries and cafeteria services.

Q: What is the biggest advantage of campus accommodation?
A: The main advantages are better security, short distance to university facilities, cleaner rooms, fewer disturbances, and less risk of eviction because of rent issues.

Q: What do students do if they are not allocated campus accommodation?
A: They stay in private hostels, flats, and rented houses in areas near campus.

Q: Which areas are commonly used for private accommodation?
A: Common areas include Chikanda, Naisi, Mpondabwino, Tholo, and Malawi Housing Corporation (MHC) flats.

Q: Why is Chikanda popular for student accommodation?
A: Chikanda is popular because it is affordable, has local markets, and has a large student population.

Q: How far is Chikanda from campus?
A: Chikanda is very close to campus, about 5 to 10 minutes away on foot.

A: Areas such as MHC flats are farther from campus, so students usually use transport such as buses or motorcycles.

Q: Are private hostels safe?
A: Private hostels are generally safe, but students are responsible for their own safety and belongings.

A: Private hostels may offer single, double, or triple rooms, but double rooms are the most common.

A: Students pay rent directly to landlords, usually in cash or by bank transfer. The university is not involved in these payments.

A: It depends on the landlord. Some private hostels have strict rules, while others are more flexible.

A: 8.1. All halls of residence are the property of the University of Malawi and the University has the prerogative to allocate any room to any student as it sees fit.
8.2. The University reserves the right of admission to the Halls of Residence and may inspect them at any time.
8.3. The University reserves the right to expel from his/her room any student who abuses or misuses a room in any Hall of Residence.
8.4. Every student shall complete a residency agreement form before occupying the room and the form shall also be used as a basis for clearance at the end of each year.
8.5. Any student who suffers injury, loss, discomfort or any other adverse consequence resulting from the conduct of any other student which infringes these Regulations should report the matter to the hall representative and, if the problem still persists, the Matron, Warden, Assistant Registrar (Student Welfare) or the Dean of Students.
8.6. Every student except those admitted on non-residential basis shall remain in residence for the full duration of the semester unless he or she is withdrawn from the Halls of Residence.
8.7. Any student who is allocated college accommodation shall not be permitted to accommodate any other person in the allocated accommodation or to sublet the whole or any part of the students' Halls of Residence for financial consideration or otherwise.
8.8. No student shall be permitted to store or use any illegal drugs or any weapons, including firearms, in the Halls of Residence and any part of any campus of the University.
8.9. No student shall consume any liquor in any Hall of Residence and respective Colleges shall prescribe additional regulations governing the sale and consumption of alcohol and the operation of pubs on their respective campuses and any other premises under their control.
8.10. Every student shall at all times ensure that lights, irons, heaters, any naked flames and any device or substance that may reasonably be expected to pose a fire hazard is switched off, extinguished or otherwise disabled immediately after use and not left unattended at any time.
8.11. Candles shall be used in Halls of Residence and other College buildings only in cases of power blackouts and should be blownoff immediately after power supply is restored.
8.12. All students have an obligation to care for all University facilities and shall be liable to compensate the University in full for any damage or loss caused to University property. Damage caused to rooms or facilities in the room shall be presumed to have been caused by the resident to whom such a room has been allocated unless the contrary is proved. Where two students share a room, responsibility for any damage or loss caused shall be shared equally between the two unless there is clear evidence that only one of them caused the damage or loss.
8.13. Students traveling on official duties shall be accommodated at the constituent College of the University of Malawi nearest to the place where the duties in question are to be executed, unless provided otherwise.
8.14. No student shall hold a party in any hall of residence and no part of any other college building or facilities may be used for a party unless this is specifically permitted in writing by the Registrar of the college in question.
- Visitors to Halls of Residence:
8.15. No student shall host any person of the opposite sex in a room in any Hall of Residence at any time between 10.00 pm and 10.00 am.
8.16. No person other than College official visitors and students from constituent Colleges for whom prior permission from the Registrar has been granted may spend a night in any hall or residence.
8.17. Every student shall be responsible for informing his or her guests of University Rules and Regulations and will be held accountable for any breach of the regulations or other inappropriate conduct by their visitors.
- Residence during Vacation:
8.18. No student shall be permitted to reside in halls of residence of any College during the period between the end of one semester and the beginning of the next unless such residence is required to enable the student, as part of the academic programme for which he or she is registered, to carry out or take part in specific tasks recommended by the relevant Head of Department and approved by the Principal of the college in question or his nominee.
8.19. Any student who is permitted to reside in a hall of residence in the period between semesters in accordance with Regulation shall be bound by these regulations.
- Health and Safety in Halls of Residence:
8.20. Every student, member of staff, visitor or any other person is prohibited from undertaking any action that would endanger the health, safety or personal security of other people in or around any Hall of residence of any college of the University including:
i. Possession of dangerous materials such as firearms, fireworks, chemicals, explosives, or potentially lethal weapons.
ii. Unauthorized entry by any person into a room other than the room to which he or she is allocated.
iii. Unauthorized possession of keys or campus identification cards or misuse of any campus locking or identification systems.
iv. Occupation of the roof of any campus building.
v. Connection of any equipment or appliance to an electrical supply in contravention of normal and reasonable safety standards.
vi. Use of immersion heaters on the University campus.
vii. Use of any appliance which draws more than one kilowatt of electrical power.
- Hall Representatives:
8.21. Every hall of residence of any college of the University shall have a Hall Representative.
8.22. A Hall Representative shall be elected by the residents of each hall of residence and shall have the responsibility to coordinate activities pertaining to the general welfare, domestic relations and discipline among students in the Halls of Residence and to represent the residents of his or her hall of residence in their contact with the college administration and other stakeholders.
- The Matron/Wardens:
8.23. Every college campus shall have a Matron and Wardens whose mandate shall be to oversee activities in the Halls of Residence and discharge specific duties which shall include, but not be limited to those related to the following areas of student life.
